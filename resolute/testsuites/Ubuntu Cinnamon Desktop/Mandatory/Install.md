*Proceed in your native language if you wish. Instructions will remain in English.*

**Boot up the image**

> *If you see the GRUB boot menu you should see the following:*
> 
> *   *'Try or Install Ubuntu Cinnamon'*
> *   *'Ubuntu Cinnamon (safe graphics)'*
> *   *'Boot from next volume'*
> *   *'UEFI Firmware Settings' (only on UEFI systems)*

> *The system boots properly and loads the live user session. Try opening and using some applications. When you are ready to install, there should be a desktop icon that says 'Install Ubuntu Cinnamon'.*

**Click on the Install Ubuntu Cinnamon button**

> *The language selection screen appears*

**Select your language and click Next**

> *The 'Accessibility' screen appears*

**Select any accessibility options, then click Next.**

> *The 'Keyboard layout' screen appears. The proposed keyboard corresponds with your keyboard*

**Select your keyboard layout and click on Next**

> *The 'Connect to the internet' screen appears*

**If you wish, you can connect to the internet. When selected, click Next**

> **Note**: If an update is available for the installer, you will be prompted with an option to update. Click the 'Update now' button and wait for the installer to update. The installer will then close and you will have to restart it.

> *The 'Type of installation' screen is displayed*

**The 'Interactive installation' option should be selected. Click Next**

> *The 'Applications' screen is displayed*

**Select which kind of installation you want, then click Next**

> *The 'Install recommended proprietary software?' screen is displayed*

**If you wish to, you may select to install third-party software or support for additional codecs. Click Next.**

> *The 'How do you want to install Ubuntu Cinnamon?' screen is displayed*

**Select your installation method and click Next**

> *At this point, there may be different options for installation depending on what is installed on your current storage device(s). Choose the option you wish to use. Manual paritioning is available under the 'Manual Installation' option, and clicking Next will bring you to the manual partitioning screen.*

> *The 'Encryption and file system' screen is displayed*

**If you wish to setup encryption, you may do so here. Otherwise, click Next**

> *The 'Create your account' screen appears*

**Input your user details and password.**

> **Note**: admin cannot be used - it is a dedicated Linux User.

> *'Require my password to log in' is shown and automatically selected. If unselected, the system will log in on startup.*

**Once you have confirmed your password, click the Next button, which will become available**

> *The 'Select your timezone' screen appears*

> *If your system is connected to the network, note the preselected timezone corresponds with your timezone and the city indicated in the text box*

> *The timezone and city displayed match your timezone and a major city from your area*

**Select your timezone, and click on the Next button**

> *The 'Review your choices' screen will appear*

> *This shows your installation information. This gives you the last chance to change anything before installation. The resulting disk partitioning will be listed.*

> **WARNING**: Changes you make to your file system will be applied if you click next. Make sure, even if you think you are sure, that you are doing what you want.

**Review your options and click 'Install'**

> *The installer slideshow begins*

**Wait for the installer to finish**

> *An 'Installation complete' screen appears*

**Click the 'Restart Now' button**

> *GUI is shut down, a prompt to remove media and press Enter appears*

**Remove the disc or boot device and press enter**

> *The machine is rebooted*

**Allow the machine to reboot**

**The system boots properly and loads into Ubuntu Cinnamon showing username selected in the login screen or on the desktop (if 'Require my password to log in' was unselected)**
